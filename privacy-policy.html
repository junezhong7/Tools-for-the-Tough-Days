Tools for the Tough Days is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains how we handle your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

1. Who we are

Tools for the Tough Days is operated by Nic Marcon, a Registered Psychologist (AHPRA). Our ABN is 69 142 299 250.

We provide a subscription-based wellbeing support platform offering clinically reviewed psychoeducational resources (including written materials and audio content) to individuals and businesses across Australia.

2. What information we collect

We may collect and hold the following types of personal information:

Information you provide directly

  • Name and email address (when subscribing or registering interest)
  • Payment information (processed securely via Stripe — we do not store card details)
  • Business name and contact details (for business subscribers)
  • Information you share when contacting us or booking a consultation

Information collected automatically

  • Device type, browser, and operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on our platform
  • IP address and general location (country/city level)
  • How you arrived at our site (referral source)

We do not collect clinical or diagnostic information. The platform does not provide psychological assessments, diagnoses, or clinical records.

3. How we collect your information

We collect personal information in the following ways:

  • Directly from you — when you subscribe, register interest, book a consultation, or contact us
  • From our platform — when you access and use our resources (usage data only, not content of what you read or listen to)
  • From third-party services — including Stripe (payment processing) and Google Analytics (website usage)
  • From email marketing — when you interact with communications sent via Vision6

4. Why we collect your information

We collect and use personal information for the following purposes:

  • To provide you with access to the platform and its resources
  • To process subscription payments securely
  • To communicate with you about your account, new content, and platform updates
  • To send marketing communications you have opted into
  • To respond to enquiries and booking requests
  • To improve the platform based on how it is used
  • To comply with our legal obligations

We will not use your personal information for any purpose that is incompatible with these stated purposes without your consent.

5. Who we share your information with

We do not sell your personal information. We may share it with trusted third-party service providers solely to operate our platform:

Payment processing

Stripe — processes subscription payments. Stripe operates under its own privacy policy and is PCI-DSS compliant. We do not store your card details. See stripe.com/au/privacy.

Email marketing

Vision6 — manages our subscriber email list and sends communications on our behalf. See Vision6 Privacy Policy.

Website analytics

Google Analytics — collects anonymised usage data to help us understand how the platform is used. Data is processed in accordance with Google's privacy policy. IP addresses are anonymised. See Google Privacy Policy.

Booking system

Calendly — used to facilitate booking of free consultation sessions. See Calendly Privacy Policy.

We do not share your personal information with any other third parties, including employers, insurers, or government agencies, except where required by law.

Business subscribers — employee privacy

If your employer subscribes to our business plan, we provide only aggregate, anonymised usage reports. Individual employees' usage, resource selections, and engagement are never disclosed to employers. What you access on this platform is private.

6. Sensitive information

Under the Privacy Act, information about a person's health is considered sensitive information and attracts a higher level of protection.

Our platform does not collect clinical or health records. However, the nature of the resources (covering topics such as anxiety, depression, trauma, and relationships) means that your use of specific content could be considered health-related in nature.

We treat all platform usage information with the highest level of care. We will never use or disclose usage information in a way that identifies you to any third party, including your employer.

7. Cookies and analytics

Our website uses cookies — small text files stored on your device — to improve your experience and understand how our site is used.

Types of cookies we use

  • Essential cookies — required for the site to function (e.g. maintaining your session)
  • Analytics cookies — Google Analytics uses these to collect anonymised usage data such as pages visited and time on site
  • Preference cookies — remember your settings and preferences

You can control or disable cookies through your browser settings. Disabling analytics cookies will not affect your ability to use the platform, but may affect our ability to improve it.

8. How we store and protect your information

We take reasonable steps to protect your personal information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access, modification, or disclosure.

These steps include:

  • HTTPS encryption across all pages of our platform
  • Secure payment processing via Stripe (PCI-DSS compliant)
  • Access controls limiting who can access subscriber data
  • Use of reputable, security-certified third-party platforms

Our platform and service providers may store data on servers located outside Australia (including in the United States). Where this occurs, we take reasonable steps to ensure the information is handled in accordance with the Australian Privacy Principles.

9. How long we keep your information

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide our services and meet our legal obligations:

  • Active subscribers — for the duration of your subscription plus seven years (consistent with standard Australian record-keeping obligations)
  • Email marketing — until you unsubscribe or request removal
  • Booking records — for the duration of your engagement with us
  • Analytics data — anonymised; retained in accordance with Google Analytics default settings

When information is no longer required, we will take reasonable steps to destroy or de-identify it securely.

10. Accessing and correcting your information

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, and to ask us to correct information that is inaccurate, out of date, incomplete, or misleading.

To make a request, please contact us using the details in Section 15. We will respond within 30 days. In some circumstances, we may be unable to provide access (for example, where doing so would impact another person's privacy), and we will explain why.

We will not charge a fee for making an access or correction request, though we may charge a reasonable fee for the cost of providing access if the request is complex.

11. Marketing communications

We may send you email communications about new resources, platform updates, and wellbeing tips. You will only receive marketing emails if you have opted in at the time of subscribing or registering interest.

You can unsubscribe at any time by clicking the unsubscribe link in any email we send, or by contacting us directly. We will process your request promptly.

12. Links to other websites

Our platform may contain links to external websites (such as crisis support services). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those websites. We encourage you to read their privacy policies before providing any personal information.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The most current version will always be available on this page, with the "Last updated" date at the top revised accordingly.

If we make material changes, we will notify active subscribers by email.

14. Complaints

If you believe we have mishandled your personal information, please contact us in the first instance using the details below. We will investigate and respond within 30 days.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC):

  • Website: www.oaic.gov.au
  • Phone: 1300 363 992
  • Post: GPO Box 5218, Sydney NSW 2001
