How to get the most from the platform
A few things worth knowing before you dive in - from someone who has sat with a lot of people in hard moments.

  • Always start with the mood check-in

    Every visit, take the mood check-in honestly - not how you think you should feel, but how you actually feel right now. A score of 1 is not failure. It's information. The platform uses it to point you toward what's genuinely helpful for today, not what you think you should be reading.

  • Let the topic pick you, not the other way around

    When you're asked what brought you here today, try to answer honestly rather than picking what sounds most acceptable. People often understate what they're dealing with. The platform is private - no one is watching. Pick what actually fits.

  • Read or listen - try both

    Every resource has a written PDF guide and an audio version. Some people absorb better through reading; others find the audio easier - especially when concentration is low, which is often when you need support most. Try the audio version on days when reading feels like too much.

  • Come back before things get bad - not just when they are

    The platform is most valuable as a regular habit, not a crisis resource. Even five minutes once a week builds a kind of mental health literacy that makes it much easier to cope when things do get hard. Most people wait too long. That's exactly what this platform is designed to change.

  • New resources are added regularly

    The library grows every month. If you've opted into email updates you'll be notified when something new arrives in an area relevant to you. If a topic you need isn't covered yet, it may well be coming - and you can always reach out and let Nic know.

  • A real person is available if you need more

    If something comes up that you want to talk through, a free 15-minute call with Nic is always available. And if you need ongoing support beyond the platform, psychology and counselling appointments can be arranged directly - just ask.
