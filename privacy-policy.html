2. Information we collect

Information you provide directly

When you register or use the Platform, you may provide:

  • Your name and email address
  • Payment details (processed by Stripe — we do not store card numbers)
  • Onboarding preferences including support style, check-in frequency, habit anchors, and quiet hours
  • Mood and wellbeing check-in data entered through the Platform

Information collected automatically

When you use the Platform, we may automatically collect:

  • Device type, operating system, and browser information
  • Pages and resources accessed, and time spent on the Platform
  • Push notification interaction data (via OneSignal)
  • General usage patterns and feature engagement

Sensitive information

Mood and wellbeing data is considered sensitive information under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth). We collect this information only with your consent, which you provide when you register and use the Platform. We handle sensitive information with additional care and do not use it for purposes other than those described in this Policy.

3. How we use your information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Create and manage your account
  • Personalise your Platform experience and tailor resource recommendations to your check-in data and preferences
  • Send scheduled check-in messages, reminders, and platform notifications in accordance with your preferences
  • Track your wellbeing patterns over time to support your progress
  • Produce anonymised, aggregate wellbeing reports for business subscribers (see Section 5)
  • Process subscription payments
  • Respond to your enquiries and provide support
  • Improve the Platform through aggregate usage analysis
  • Meet our legal and regulatory obligations

We do not use your personal information for direct marketing by third parties, and we do not sell your data.

4. Grounds for collecting sensitive information

We collect mood and wellbeing data on the basis of your explicit consent, provided when you register and confirmed each time you complete a check-in. You may withdraw consent at any time by requesting account deactivation. Withdrawal of consent will result in the deletion of your personal data in accordance with Section 9 of this Policy.

5. Business subscribers and aggregate reporting

Individual employee data is never shared with your employer.

If you access the Platform through a workplace subscription, your employer receives only anonymised, aggregate reports showing workforce-level wellbeing trends.

These reports are designed so that no individual can be identified. Specifically:

  • Any metric derived from fewer than five individual responses is suppressed from the report
  • Reports show directional trends only — not individual scores, names, or identifiers
  • This suppression rule is stated explicitly in every business report provided to employers

Business subscribers (employers) agree to these data handling terms as part of their subscription agreement. They are not permitted to attempt to identify individuals from aggregate reports.

6. Data storage and security

All personal data collected by the Platform is stored securely in Microsoft Azure, hosted in Australia where available.

We apply industry-standard security practices including:

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS) and at rest
  • Access controls limiting data access to authorised personnel only
  • Regular security reviews aligned with the Australian Cyber Security Centre (ACSC) Essential Eight framework
  • Cyber and IT liability insurance coverage (currently being finalised)

Despite these measures, no system is completely secure. If you believe your account has been compromised, please contact us immediately at tttd@emotionalbalance.com.au.

8. Disclosure required by law

We may disclose your personal information if required to do so by law, court order, or where we reasonably believe disclosure is necessary to protect the safety of any person, including in response to a mandatory reporting obligation.

As a registered psychologist, Nic Marcon is subject to mandatory reporting obligations under applicable legislation. These obligations apply to the in-house counselling service, not to Platform use. Platform use does not create a clinical relationship and does not trigger these obligations in relation to Platform data.

9. Data retention and deletion

Retention period

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active. If you cancel your subscription, your account and data are retained for 90 days before permanent deletion, to allow for reactivation if needed.

Requesting deletion

You may request deletion of your personal data at any time by contacting us at tttd@emotionalbalance.com.au. We will confirm receipt and complete the deletion within 30 days, subject to any legal retention obligations (for example, financial records required under tax law).

Aggregate data

Anonymised aggregate data derived from your usage (for example, workforce-level trend data in business reports) cannot be deleted as it contains no personal information and cannot be attributed to you.

10. Your privacy rights

Under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Request deletion of your personal information (subject to legal retention obligations)
  • Withdraw consent to the collection of sensitive information
  • Make a complaint about how we handle your personal information

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at tttd@emotionalbalance.com.au. We will respond within 30 days.

11. Complaints

If you have a complaint about how we have handled your personal information, please contact us first at tttd@emotionalbalance.com.au. We will acknowledge your complaint within five business days and work to resolve it promptly.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC):

  • Website: oaic.gov.au
  • Phone: 1300 363 992
  • Post: GPO Box 5218, Sydney NSW 2001

12. Cookies and analytics

The Platform uses Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity to collect anonymised usage data. This helps us understand how the Platform is used so we can improve it. Data collected by these services is aggregated and does not identify individual users. Microsoft Clarity may record session interactions such as clicks and scrolls in anonymised form to help us identify usability issues.

The Platform may use cookies or local storage to maintain your session and preferences. These are functional and do not track you across other websites.

13. Children

The Platform is intended for adults aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under the age of 18. If we become aware that a person under 18 has provided personal information, we will delete it promptly.

14. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify active subscribers of material changes by email at least 14 days before they take effect.

The current version of this Policy will always be available at toolsforthetoughdays.com.au/privacy, with the "Last updated" date revised accordingly.
